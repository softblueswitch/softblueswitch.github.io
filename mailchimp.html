Did someone say Mail Chimp?

It's not every day that you get invited to an interactive party to create digital playthings that are visceral, unexpected, delightful and downright weird - it's alwavs a breath of fresh air to have a brand willing to do things differently. In 2014, MailChimp struck marketing gold when a 13-year-old girl mispronounced their name 'MailKimp' on the cult podcast Serial. This inspired a collection of cultural activations that captured people's imaginations around the world. From interactive music videos to whale synthesisers, we crafted nine digital experiences, creating an eclectic multiverse where everything sounds like "MailChimp.


www.resn.co.nz/mailchimp

WhaleSynth was another illegitimate child of MailChimp, this site ran like an app inside a mobile browser and made spooky whale noises. I was the interactive producer on this and worked closely with developers and designers to deliver a product that the client loved.



FailChips is a play on the mispronunciation of "MailChimp", for whom this site was produced. The idea was that these chips are "fails" because they are already crumbled to tiny fragments, the crumbs that you find in the bottom of your bag of chips, where all the flavour and salt has concentrated. These chips consisted of just those crumbs and it was possible to buy actual bags of them in stores across the US. I worked as the interactive producer for this site, fielding clients decisions through our partner agency, Droga 5 in New York.
